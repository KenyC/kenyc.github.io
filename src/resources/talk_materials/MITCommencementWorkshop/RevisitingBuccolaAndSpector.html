Revisiting Buccola & Spector

Keny Chatain (ENS/PSL)1

Goal:

  • Discuss a generalization proposed by Buccola & Spector about the interaction between modified numerals and collectives predicates.
  • Discuss problematic cases for the generalization
  • Show that the patterns they discuss are in fact subsumed by the gather/numerous distinction.

Distribution of the upper-bounded reading

Empirical generalization

Typically, it is thought that less than numerals set an upper-bound on some quantity.

(1) a. Less than 10 dolphins jumped through the hoop.
b. Upper-bounded TCs:
\(\max \left\lbrace n \,\middle|\, n\text{ dolphins jumped through the hoop}\right\rbrace < 10\)

Dual behavior with collective predicates: surround vs gather. Buccola and Spector (2016) find that certain collective predicates do not have upper-bounded inferences:

(2) a. Less than 10 soldiers surrounded the castle.
b. Existential TCs:
\(\exists X,\ |X| < 10 \land X\text{ surrounded the castle}\)

But others do:

(3) a. Less than 15 protestors gathered in Tahrir Square.
b. Upper-bounded TCs:
\(\max \left\lbrace n \,\middle|\, n\text{ gathered in Tahrir Square}\right\rbrace < 15\)

They argue that weakly distributive predicates are the ones that yield upper-bounded inferences.

(4) Weak distributivity (to be revised):
\(P\) is weakly distributive iff \[\forall X, P(X) \land |X| \geq 2 \rightarrow \left(\exists Y, Y \prec X \land |Y| = 2 \land P(y)\right)\] “If P is true of a group \(X\) with at least 2 members, it is true of a subgroup of \(X\) with two members.”

Buccola and Spector’s proposal

They have many proposals. Here, we discuss one of the proposals, labelled LMaxInf in the paper2.

Structure-wise, they assume less than 10 is a numeral quantifier that can scope out of the DP.

(5) a. Less than 10 dolphins jumped through the hoop.
b. less than 10 λn. \(\exists\) [n dolphins] jumped through the hoop

They model the semantics of modified numerals around a maximal informativity operator:

(6) \([\![ \text{less than 10} ]\!](P)(w) = \exists n < 10, \text{max-inf}(P)(w)(n)\)
“some maximally informative number is less than 10”

A number \(n\) is maximally informative wrt \(P\) iff \(P(n)(w)\) and \(P(n)\) is not entailed by any \(P(m)\) for \(m\neq n\) such that \(P(m)(w)\).

Let’s see what this entry predicts when the numeral takes high scope:

(7) LF: less than 10 λn. \(\exists\) [n dolphins] VP
a. When VP is jumped
… ⇒ 12 dolphins jumped ⇒ 11 dolphins jumped ⇒ 10 dolphins jumped ⇒ …
b. When VP is surround the castle
… ⇎ 12 soldiers surrounded the castle ⇎ 11 soldiers surrounded the castle ⇎ 10 soldiers surrounded the castle ⇎ …

In (7a), informativity order is the same as numerical order: the sentence has an upper-bound reading.
In (7b), every number is maximally informative: no upper-bound.

gather’s entailment patterns are the same as jumped:

(8) … ⇒ 12 protestors gathered ⇒ 11 protestors gathered ⇒ 10 protestors gathered ⇒ …

Note I: the property of weak distributivity as stated by Buccola & Spector is not what guarantees upper-bounded reading in their system. Rather, the following property seems important:

(9) Weak distributivity (new):
\(P\) is weakly distributive iff \[\forall X, \forall n \geq T, P(X) \land |X| = n \rightarrow \left(\exists Y, |Y| = n - 1 \land P(Y)\right)\] “If P is true of a group of n members with n sufficiently large, it is true of a group of \((n - 1)\) members.”

Note II: They also discuss a DP-internal scope for less than 10, which they need a pragmatic rule to rule out. This won’t play a role in what’s to come.

Reinterpreting the generalization

Empirical problems

Buccola and Spector’s generalization:

weakly distributive predicate \(\Leftrightarrow\) upper-bounded interpretation

We see a double dissociation:

  1. some predicates yield upper-bounded readings but they are not weakly distributive
  2. some predicates don’t yield upper-bounded inference while being weakly distributive

Upper-bounded interpretations with non-weakly distributive predicates
For A., predicates like hold hands or fit together are not weakly distributive

(10) Kuhn (2020)
a. Less than 6 people are holding hands.
b. Less than 16 pieces in this jigsaw puzzle fit together.

(13) … ⇎ 6 people are holding hands ⇎ 5 people are holding hands ⇎ 4 people are holding hands ⇎ …
(Or perhaps better se tiennent par la main “SE hold by hand”3)

Weakly distributive predicates with no upper-bounded interpretations
Point B. can be made with predicates like cheap or weigh less than 5kgs. There is a slight confound in that these predicates have multiple construals:

(14) These bottles weigh less than 5 kgs.
a. Distributive: each weigh less than 5 kgs.
b. Collective: total weight is less than 5 kgs.

I take the line that (for these predicates at least,) there is a genuine ambiguity. One argument comes from Bar-Lev (2020):

(15) a. Target:
_ These bottles are cheap.
_ That’s not true! Oh you meant individually.
b. Control:
_ You went to Boston.
_ # That’s not true! Oh you meant by plane.

I’m only interested in the collective meaning of the predicate. Under their collective reading, these predicates are weakly distributive:

(16) a. … ⇒ 12 bottles are cheap ⇒ 11 bottles are cheap ⇒ 10 bottles are cheap ⇒ …
b. … ⇒ 12 bottles weigh less than 5 kgs ⇒ 11 bottles weigh less than 5 kgs ⇒ 10 bottles weigh less than 5 kgs ⇒ …

Yet, there does not seem to be an upper-bounded collective reading with numerals like less than

(17) a. Less than 10 bottles are cheap.
≠ any group of 10 bottles or more is not cheap
b. Less than 10 bottles weigh less than 15 kg.
≠ any group of 10 bottles or more weighs 15kg or more

Another angle: gather-type and numerous-type

What do held hands, gather and fit together have in common?
They belong to the class of gather-type predicates

Gather-type predicates are predicates that can yield a collective meaning in combination with all:

(18) a. All the protestors gathered in Tahrir Square.
b. All the pupils held hands.
c. All the pieces fit together.

By contrast, numerous-type predicates only yield distributive readings:

(19) a. # All the soldiers are numerous.
b. All the bottles weigh 5kg.
≠ total weight is 5kg

It seems part of Buccola and Spector’s generalization can be rephrased as follows:

(20) Upper-bounded less than modified numerals have the same distributional requirements as all, i.e. they only yield collective readings with gather-type predicates.

Wait! What about surround the castle? It seems fine with all:

(21) COCA examples
a. ✓ All the men surround the hole with their rifles pointing at it.
b. ✓ All the nations surrounded me, but in the name of the Lord I cut them off.

That’s because the gather/numerous is actually a 3-way distinction (Kuhn (2020)):

(22) a. All the protestors gathered in Tahrir Square. (gather-type)
b. ?All the children built the boat. (Dowty-type)
c. *All the books in this shop are cheap. (numerous-type)

This is probably not a matter of “dialects” (Kuhn 2020), as has been said (Winter2000?). Judgments get crispier with non-partitive quantifiers, cleanly setting apart gather-type from Dowty-type:

(23) a. Most boys gathered.
b. *Most boys built the boat.
c. *Most boys surrounded the castle.

surround the castle is probably Dowty-type:

(24) * Most soldiers surrounded the castle.

Directions for analysis

The original generalization by Buccola & Spector came with a principled analysis.

Making sense of the new generalization will require answering: the following question: what makes a gather-type predicate ? Authors on this issue share an intuition ; but it hasn’t been easy to spell it out.

Underlying Intuition: gather-type predicates have downward inferences (aka “subentailments”).

(25) Numerous-type predicates
a. The apples are numerous.
b. The bottles cost $10.
(26) Gather-type predicates
a. The protestors gathered.
b. The professors agreed.
c. The laureates sat together.
d. The pupils were holding hands.

Gather-type predicates as eventful stratified reference (Kuhn 2020). Every event in the denotation of the predicate of can be decomposed in a sum of “small” events in the denotation of the predicate.

(27) \(\forall e, P(e) \rightarrow \exists E, \bigoplus E = e \land \forall e'\in E, P(e') \land \mu(e') \leq \epsilon\)
where \(\epsilon\) is “small”

To the extent that an event of 6 bottles weighing less than 10kg is not composed of smaller events of 2 bottles weighing less than 10kg, we correctly rule out the undesirable meanings.

Problem

Conceptual challenges: why would the semantics of quantifiers be sensitive to this property?

(28) \([\![\text{all}]\!]= \lambda P. \lambda Q: \mathtt{StratRef}(Q). Q(\iota P)\)
  • In Champollion and Kuhn’s work, the stratified property is listed as a presupposition of the quantifier
  • It is an essentially arbitrary restriction on a meaning that would otherwise be compatible with all predicates.
  • Why not a language where quantifiers only combine with numerous-type predicates?
(29) An innovative revolutionary new quantifier: yall
\([\![\text{yall}]\!]= \lambda P. \lambda Q: \neg\mathtt{StratRef}(Q). Q(\iota P)\)

Empirical challenges: Various manipulations that disrupt the stratification do not disrupt gather-typeness. For instance, adding in an adjunct

(30) All the pilgrims sat together in one large circle.

Stratification isn’t even closed under conjunction, but gather-typeness is

(31) Most people in this picture are both kissing and holding hands

Conclusion

Conclusion

  • Buccola & Spector’s generalization can be seen as showing that the gather/numerous distinction is active for modified numerals.
  • Modified numerals simply have an “escape reading”, the existential reading
  • Idea:
    • gather-type predicate shouldn’t be characterized extensionally (what inferential properties they have)
    • they should perhaps be described intensionally (what they’re made up of)
    • In particular, gather-type predicates frequently seem to imply notions of reciprocity, sameness, etc. A closer inspection of these semantic concepts might explain their co-occurrence with plural quantifiers (Kamp and Reyle 2013).
    • Compare this with the pseudo-gather-type predicates like weigh less than 5 kg.
