Outside the world of linguistics

I had the chance to travel a lot in my life, and I love discovering new places. My favorite city in the world remains London although I'm pretty sure I would change my mind if I were to live there. I went to post-revolutionary Cairo during the Soccer World Cup, which is a very good memory. I did part of the Camino de Santiago in Spain (purely for hiking purposes).

I like reading fiction and non-fiction. I have a snobbish taste for French classical theater. I like puzzles, enigmas of all kind and brain twisting things.

I run, I play badminton, I bike, I hike, etc.

The question every linguist is asked: how many languages do you speak? Here is a (too?) generous count:

  • French (less and less)
  • English (not getting any better)
  • Spanish (immersion activates me)
  • Arabic ("he sounds like a Soviet general")
  • German (I master embeddings, so technically, I can form infinitely many sentences)
  • Japanese (one sentence: Hanako eats pizza)
  • Russian (Da and Niet but I can't spell them)
  • Latin (dura lex sed lex)
  • Ancient Greek (ta zoa trekei)
  • LSF (the sign for buying)...

Yes, my first name is not a French name. I've actually never met any person with that name in France. If that person exists, he is certainly spelled differently. There's no special reason for my name.

I'm addicted to candies. Certainly the easiest way to bribe me...
